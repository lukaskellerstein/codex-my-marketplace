# Curated Font Pairings

A reference of 22 tested font pairings organized by mood. Each pairing specifies a heading font and a body font, with guidance on weights, sizing, and emotional effect. All fonts listed are available on Google Fonts unless noted otherwise.

---

## Authoritative

Pairings that convey trust, expertise, and institutional credibility.

### 1. Playfair Display + Source Sans 3

- **Emotional effect** — Established authority with approachable warmth
- **Best for** — Law firms, financial services, universities, editorial magazines
- **Weight strategy** — Heading: Bold (700) or Black (900); Body: Regular (400) with Semibold (600) for emphasis
- **Size contrast** — 3:1 ratio (e.g., 48px heading / 16px body)
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — Playfair Display's high-contrast serifs command attention and signal tradition, while Source Sans 3's open letterforms keep body text effortlessly readable at any size.

### 2. Fraunces + Inter

- **Emotional effect** — Confident expertise with a hint of personality
- **Best for** — Modern consultancies, craft brands with authority, thought leadership blogs
- **Weight strategy** — Heading: Black (900) with optical size axis if variable; Body: Regular (400)
- **Size contrast** — 3:1 ratio
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — Fraunces brings soft, wonky serifs that feel human and distinctive, while Inter's neutral precision grounds the layout and ensures clarity in longer passages.

### 3. DM Serif Display + DM Sans

- **Emotional effect** — Polished and cohesive professionalism
- **Best for** — Corporate websites, product landing pages, annual reports
- **Weight strategy** — Heading: Regular (400, the only weight); Body: Regular (400) with Medium (500) for UI elements
- **Size contrast** — 2.5:1 ratio
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — Designed as a matched pair by the same foundry, they share underlying proportions and x-height, creating seamless visual harmony without effort.

### 4. Libre Baskerville + Karla

- **Emotional effect** — Traditional gravitas with friendly accessibility
- **Best for** — Publishing houses, nonprofit organizations, academic journals
- **Weight strategy** — Heading: Bold (700); Body: Regular (400) with Bold (700) for inline emphasis
- **Size contrast** — 2.5:1 ratio
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — Libre Baskerville channels the reliability of Baskerville's 18th-century letterforms, while Karla's slightly quirky grotesque shapes add contemporary friendliness to the body.

## Editorial

Pairings designed for long-form reading, storytelling, and publishing.

### 13. Instrument Serif + Instrument Sans

- **Emotional effect** — Elegant, intentional, and quietly sophisticated
- **Best for** — Online magazines, literary journals, premium blogs, longform storytelling
- **Weight strategy** — Heading: Regular (400, the only serif weight); Body: Regular (400) with Medium (500) for bylines
- **Size contrast** — 2.5:1 ratio
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — As a matched serif and sans pair, they share identical metrics and proportions, creating a seamless editorial system where headlines and body text feel like they belong to the same voice.

### 14. Playfair Display + Lora

- **Emotional effect** — Rich, layered, and deeply literary
- **Best for** — Book review sites, literary magazines, cultural commentary, poetry publications
- **Weight strategy** — Heading: Bold (700); Body: Regular (400) with Italic for pull quotes
- **Size contrast** — 2.5:1 ratio
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — This double-serif pairing works because Playfair Display's high contrast sets it apart as display type, while Lora's more moderate contrast and calligraphic curves make it comfortable for sustained reading.

### 15. Cormorant Garamond + Proza Libre

- **Size contrast** — 3:1 ratio (Cormorant is designed to be used large)
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — Cormorant Garamond's delicate, high-contrast strokes evoke classical typography at display sizes, while Proza Libre's wider proportions and friendly serifs maintain warmth and legibility in body text.

### 16. Newsreader + Inter

- **Emotional effect** — Journalistic credibility with digital-first clarity
- **Best for** — News websites, investigative journalism, newsletters, opinion platforms
- **Weight strategy** — Heading: ExtraBold (800); Body: Regular (400) with Medium (500) for section labels

## Technical

Pairings for developer tools, documentation, and data-driven interfaces.

### 17. JetBrains Mono + Space Grotesk

- **Emotional effect** — Code-native precision with geometric clarity
- **Best for** — Developer documentation, code editors, technical blogs, API references
- **Weight strategy** — Heading/Code: Regular (400) or Bold (700); Body: Regular (400) with Medium (500) for navigation
- **Size contrast** — 1.5:1 ratio (monospace pairs work better with less contrast)
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — JetBrains Mono's increased x-height and distinctive ligatures make code blocks scannable and beautiful, while Space Grotesk's monospaced heritage creates natural visual kinship in surrounding prose.

### 18. IBM Plex Mono + IBM Plex Sans

- **Emotional effect** — Unified, systematic, and enterprise-grade
- **Best for** — Enterprise documentation, design systems, data dashboards, infrastructure tools
- **Weight strategy** — Code: Regular (400); Body: Regular (400) with SemiBold (600) for headings
- **Size contrast** — 1.5:1 ratio
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — As members of the same superfamily, IBM Plex Mono and Sans share identical vertical metrics, x-heights, and design philosophy, creating a perfectly integrated typographic system for technical content.

### 19. Space Mono + DM Sans

- **Emotional effect** — Retro-tech personality with modern grounding
- **Best for** — Creative coding projects, generative art sites, experimental tech portfolios
- **Weight strategy** — Heading: Bold (700); Body: Regular (400) with Medium (500) for labels
- **Size contrast** — 2:1 ratio
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — Space Mono's quirky, wide letterforms bring personality typically missing from technical typography, while DM Sans smooths out the reading experience for anything beyond short headings.

### 20. Fira Code + Fira Sans

- **Emotional effect** — Developer-friendly and systematically cohesive
- **Best for** — Open-source documentation, developer blogs, CLI tool sites, code tutorials
- **Weight strategy** — Code: Regular (400) with SemiBold (600); Body: Regular (400) with Medium (500) for headings
- **Size contrast** — 1.5:1 ratio
- **Google Fonts** — Yes (Fira Code and Fira Sans both available)
- **Why they work** — Fira Code's programming ligatures make code expressive and scannable, and since both fonts descend from the same Fira family, they share proportions that keep mono and sans text visually aligned.

## Luxurious

Pairings that signal premium quality, refinement, and exclusivity.

- **Weight strategy** — Heading: Bold (700) or SemiBold (600); Body: Light (300) or Regular (400) with Medium (500) for navigation

- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — Cormorant Garamond's thin, high-contrast strokes evoke the refinement of classical French typography, while Montserrat's geometric uniformity provides a clean, modern counterpoint that keeps the design from feeling dated.

### 22. Bodoni Moda + DM Sans

- **Emotional effect** — High-fashion contrast with accessible warmth
- **Best for** — Fashion editorial, luxury e-commerce, beauty brands, gallery exhibitions
- **Weight strategy** — Heading: Bold (700) or Black (900); Body: Regular (400) with Medium (500) for CTAs
- **Size contrast** — 3.5:1 ratio
- **Google Fonts** — Yes
- **Why they work** — Bodoni Moda's extreme thick-thin contrast is the typographic language of high fashion and prestige, while DM Sans prevents the design from becoming inaccessible by providing warm, open body text that invites rather than intimidates.
