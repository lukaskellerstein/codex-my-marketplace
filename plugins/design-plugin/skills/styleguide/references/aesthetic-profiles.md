# Aesthetic Profiles

Aesthetic profiles are pre-defined design directions that capture a cohesive visual identity across typography, color, spacing, imagery, and motion. Use them as a starting point when building a styleguide -- pick the profile that best matches the project's brand personality, audience, and goals, then customize the specifics from there.

---

### Swiss Precision

**Description**
Clean, grid-based, rational design rooted in the Swiss/International Typographic Style of the mid-20th century. Every element has a clear purpose and a mathematically defined position. Communication is direct, unambiguous, and confident.

**Best for**
Data-heavy applications, scientific and research platforms, enterprise dashboards, transit and wayfinding systems, government digital services.

**Typography direction**
Grotesque or neo-grotesque sans-serifs (Helvetica Neue, Aktiv Grotesk, Inter). Medium-weight headings, regular-weight body text. Strict typographic scale with consistent line heights. Uppercase is used purposefully for labels and navigation.

**Color mood**
Restrained palettes -- often monochromatic with a single accent hue. High contrast black-on-white foundations. Colors are functional rather than decorative. When used, accents lean toward primary hues (red, blue, yellow) applied with precision.

**Spacing density**
Balanced to spacious. Generous margins, strict grid adherence. Consistent gutters create rhythm. Whitespace is treated as an active design element, not empty space.

**Imagery style**
Photography is documentary, objective, and sharply focused. Illustrations, when present, are geometric and diagrammatic. Iconography is simple, mono-weight line icons or filled geometric shapes.

**Motion approach**
Minimal and functional. Transitions are linear or ease-out, short duration (150-250ms). No decorative animation. Motion serves to clarify state changes and spatial relationships.

**Reference sites**
- swiss.com (Swiss International Air Lines)
- vitra.com
- sbb.ch (Swiss Federal Railways)

### Neo-Brutalist

**Description**
Raw, bold, and intentionally rough. Visible structure and exposed mechanics replace polish with authenticity. Borders are thick, shadows are hard, and nothing pretends to be anything it is not. It is the anti-trend trend.

**Best for**
Creative portfolios, indie products, experimental marketing sites, developer tools, zines and editorial experiments, Web3 and crypto projects.

**Typography direction**
Heavy, high-impact typefaces -- often monospaced or slab-serifs (Space Mono, Courier, Roboto Slab). Extra-bold weights for headings. Body text can be monospaced to reinforce the raw, code-like aesthetic. Sizes tend toward extremes: very large or very small.

**Color mood**
High saturation, often clashing. Black and white foundations with electric accent colors (hot pink, acid yellow, bright cyan). Backgrounds may be off-white or stark primary colors. No gradients -- flat and unapologetic.

**Spacing density**
Compact to balanced. Thick visible borders and box outlines create a sense of density. Padding is generous inside containers, but containers themselves are packed tightly. Deliberate asymmetry is common.

**Imagery style**
Collage-like compositions, pixel art, hand-drawn elements, or intentionally low-fidelity graphics. Photography is often desaturated, dithered, or treated with harsh filters. Stickers and overlapping elements are welcome.

**Motion approach**
Expressive and unexpected. Abrupt transitions, exaggerated hover states, intentional jank. Marquee scrolling text and glitch effects are on-brand. Motion draws attention to the unconventional nature of the design.

**Reference sites**
- brutalist-web.design
- gumroad.com
- poolsuite.net

### Japanese Zen

**Description**
Minimalist design inspired by traditional Japanese aesthetics -- wabi-sabi, ma (negative space), and kanso (simplicity). Every element breathes. Restraint is the defining principle, and the absence of elements communicates as strongly as their presence.

**Best for**
Wellness and mindfulness apps, luxury hospitality, tea and food brands, architecture portfolios, meditation and health platforms.

**Typography direction**
Clean sans-serifs with subtle warmth (Noto Sans, Source Han Sans for CJK, or thin-weight geometric sans). Light to regular weights dominate. Generous tracking in headings. Minimal use of bold -- hierarchy is achieved through size and space rather than weight.

**Color mood**
Muted, desaturated, and natural. Warm off-whites, stone grays, and soft blacks. Accent colors are drawn from nature: moss green, clay, washed indigo, pale cherry blossom. Palettes are narrow -- typically 2-3 colors.

**Spacing density**
Spacious. Extremely generous whitespace is the defining feature. Elements float in open compositions. Line heights are tall, margins are wide, and the overall density is low. Empty space is the primary design tool.

**Imagery style**
High-quality, contemplative photography with shallow depth of field or soft focus. Subjects are natural -- textures of wood, stone, water, fabric. Illustrations are ink-brush or woodblock-inspired. No clutter.

**Motion approach**
Subtle and meditative. Slow fades (400-800ms), gentle parallax, and gradual reveals. Easing is soft and organic. Motion should feel like watching steam rise from tea -- unhurried and calming.

**Reference sites**
- aman.com (Aman Resorts)
- muji.com
- teamlab.art

### Playful Tech

**Description**
Fun, colorful, and approachable. Technology should feel like a toy you want to pick up, not a tool you have to learn. Rounded shapes, friendly illustrations, and cheerful palettes lower barriers and invite exploration.

**Best for**
Consumer SaaS products, educational platforms, children's apps, onboarding flows, social tools, productivity apps targeting creative users.

**Typography direction**
Rounded, geometric sans-serifs (Nunito, Poppins, Quicksand, Plus Jakarta Sans). Medium to semi-bold weights. Headings are large and friendly. Body text is comfortable -- 16-18px with generous line height.

**Color mood**
Vibrant and saturated, but not overwhelming. Multi-color palettes with a clear primary and 3-4 supporting hues. Pastels mixed with punchy accents. Gradients are common -- soft, multi-stop blends that feel energetic. Backgrounds are often light with colorful accents.

**Spacing density**
Balanced. Comfortable padding, rounded corners (8-16px radius), and card-based layouts. Nothing feels cramped. Consistent spacing creates a sense of order even when the palette is energetic.

**Imagery style**
Custom illustrations in a flat or semi-flat style with rounded shapes and friendly characters. Isometric scenes for feature explanations. Photography is bright, candid, and diverse. Iconography is filled, rounded, and often two-toned.

**Motion approach**
Expressive and delightful. Bouncy easing, spring physics, playful micro-interactions on hover and click. Confetti on success states. Loading animations are fun and branded. Motion makes the product feel alive.

**Reference sites**
- notion.so
- duolingo.com

### Editorial Luxury

**Description**
Magazine-quality digital design where content reigns supreme. Beautiful typographic compositions, dramatic layouts, and sophisticated restraint. Every page feels like opening a spread in Vogue or Monocle. The design serves the content, never competes with it.

**Best for**
Fashion and lifestyle brands, long-form editorial sites, luxury e-commerce, art galleries, cultural institutions, high-end restaurants and hotels.

**Typography direction**
Elegant serifs for headings (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, EB Garamond, freight). High-contrast typefaces with thin strokes and dramatic weight variation. Body text in a refined sans-serif or transitional serif. Italic styles used generously for pull quotes and emphasis. Large type sizes, tight tracking in headings.

**Color mood**
Restrained and sophisticated. Black, charcoal, and cream dominate. Accent colors are singular and intentional -- deep burgundy, navy, forest green, or gold. Muted tones over bright ones. The palette whispers rather than shouts.

**Spacing density**
Spacious with dramatic variation. Oversized margins alternate with full-bleed imagery. Asymmetric layouts with intentional tension. Vertical rhythm is carefully considered. The overall feel is curated, not templated.

**Imagery style**
High-resolution, art-directed photography is central. Images are large, often full-bleed or dramatically cropped. Black-and-white photography used alongside color. Editorial styling -- every image tells a story. Minimal iconography.

**Motion approach**
Subtle and cinematic. Slow, graceful transitions. Parallax scrolling done tastefully. Text reveals with gentle upward fades. Scroll-driven animations that enhance the narrative. Nothing flashy -- everything is controlled.

**Reference sites**
- monocle.com
- celinne.com (Celine)
- kinfolk.com

### Nordic Minimal

**Description**
Scandinavian simplicity where function dictates form. Designs are clean, honest, and unadorned. There is warmth in the restraint -- achieved through natural color tones, balanced compositions, and a deep respect for usability over decoration.

**Best for**
Furniture and homeware brands, architecture firms, sustainable and eco-friendly products, fintech, healthcare, Scandinavian markets.

**Typography direction**
Geometric or humanist sans-serifs with a clean, neutral character (Söhne, GT Walsheim, DM Sans, Karla). Regular to medium weights. Headings are understated -- hierarchy through size, not emphasis. Text is functional and readable above all else.

**Color mood**
Muted and natural. Soft whites, warm grays, pale wood tones, and muted sage or dusty blue. The palette feels like natural daylight in a well-designed room. Accents are soft -- terracotta, slate blue, or warm sand. Low saturation throughout.

**Spacing density**
Spacious but not extravagant. Ample padding, consistent grids, and balanced proportions. Components feel airy and well-organized. Nothing competes for attention. Spacing serves clarity.

**Imagery style**
Lifestyle photography with natural light. Clean backgrounds, simple compositions. Products are shown in context -- real homes, real people, natural environments. Illustration is minimal and geometric when present. Iconography is thin-line and functional.

**Motion approach**
Minimal and purposeful. Smooth transitions with soft easing (200-400ms). No decorative animation. Micro-interactions are subtle -- a quiet acknowledgment of user action, never a performance.

**Reference sites**
- hay.dk
- iittala.com
- nordiskgalleriet.se

### Warm Organic

**Description**
Earthy, handcrafted, and human. This profile rejects the cold precision of digital defaults in favor of warmth and texture. It feels like it was made by real hands -- imperfect, natural, and inviting.

**Best for**
Artisan food and beverage brands, farmers markets, handmade goods, wellness and natural beauty, community organizations, non-profits, bakeries and cafes.

**Typography direction**
Warm serifs or hand-drawn influenced typefaces (Lora, Merriweather, Fraunces, or a carefully chosen script for accents). Body text in a readable serif or warm humanist sans. Mixed typographic styles are welcome -- they add to the handcrafted feel. Weights tend toward regular to semi-bold.

**Color mood**
Warm, earthy, and natural. Terracotta, ochre, olive green, warm cream, deep brown, burnt sienna. The palette feels like it was mixed from natural pigments. Saturations are moderate -- rich but not artificial. Backgrounds are warm-toned papers or subtle textures.

**Spacing density**
Balanced to spacious. Loose, slightly informal layouts. Overlapping elements and organic positioning are welcome. Grid structure exists but is not rigid. The overall impression is comfortable and inviting, like a well-worn kitchen table.

**Imagery style**
Warm, textured photography with natural light. Overhead food shots, close-up textures of materials, hands at work. Illustrations lean hand-drawn, watercolor, or woodcut. Paper textures, grain, and slight imperfections are intentional. Iconography has visible stroke weight and a hand-drawn quality.

**Motion approach**
Gentle and natural. Soft fades, slight parallax on scroll. No mechanical or robotic feeling. Motion should feel like turning a page or unfolding a cloth -- organic and unhurried. Duration on the longer side (300-600ms).

**Reference sites**
- ottolenghi.co.uk
- grfranco.com (Gran Franco)
- toasttab.com/local-restaurants

### Dark Premium

**Description**
Dark backgrounds set the stage for high-contrast content that demands attention. This profile conveys exclusivity, sophistication, and technological prowess. Elements emerge from darkness like objects in a spotlight.

**Best for**
Automotive brands, gaming platforms, pro creative tools, fintech and trading platforms, music streaming, nightlife and entertainment, high-end electronics.

**Typography direction**
Clean, modern sans-serifs that read well on dark backgrounds (Inter, SF Pro, Outfit, General Sans). Thin to medium weights for body text -- thin strokes look elegant against dark. Headings can go bold or ultra-light for contrast. White or near-white text on dark surfaces.

**Color mood**
Dark foundations -- near-black, deep charcoal, or dark navy. Accents are vivid and luminous: electric blue, neon green, amber, or violet. Gradients with depth -- glowing effects, subtle radial gradients behind key elements. Metallics (gold, silver) for premium signaling. Color is used sparingly but with high impact.

**Spacing density**
Balanced to spacious. Elements benefit from generous spacing on dark backgrounds -- overcrowding feels claustrophobic. Cards and sections are clearly delineated with subtle borders, elevation, or background variation. Padding is generous.

**Imagery style**
High-contrast, dramatically lit photography. Products shot on black with controlled lighting. Abstract 3D renders, glass and metal materials, glowing particles. Minimal flat illustration -- when used, it is detailed and dimensional. UI screenshots with glow effects.

**Motion approach**
Cinematic and polished. Smooth reveals from darkness, glow pulses, subtle particle effects. Loading states with ambient animation. Hover states with luminous transitions. Motion enhances the premium feeling -- nothing cheap or bouncy.

**Reference sites**
- linear.app
- apple.com/macbook-pro
- porsche.com

### Retro Nostalgic

**Description**
Vintage-inspired design that draws from specific past eras -- 1950s Americana, 1970s groovy, 1980s neon, or 1990s web. The charm lies in recognizable visual cues that trigger warm memories while still functioning as modern digital design.

**Best for**
Food and drink brands with heritage, music and vinyl culture, barbershops and vintage shops, retro gaming, event promotions, themed restaurants and bars.

**Typography direction**
Era-specific display faces -- slab serifs, rounded gothics, psychedelic scripts, or pixel fonts depending on the target decade. Mixing two eras is risky; commit to one. Body text should still be readable -- pair a vintage display face with a clean workhorse serif or sans. Bold weights and ALL CAPS are common in headings.

**Color mood**
Palette depends on the era. 1950s: pastels (mint, pink, butter). 1970s: harvest gold, burnt orange, avocado green, brown. 1980s: hot pink, electric cyan, purple, black. 1990s: teal, purple, primary colors, gray. Common thread: colors feel distinctly dated in an intentional, affectionate way.

**Spacing density**
Compact to balanced. Vintage design tends to fill the frame. Borders, badges, and ornamental dividers are common. Layouts may mimic print formats -- newspaper columns, poster compositions, or packaging labels.

**Imagery style**
Halftone patterns, duotone treatments, vintage filters. Photography is styled to match the era -- film grain, specific color grading. Illustrations reference period styles: mid-century modern, psychedelic, early vector art, or pixel art. Badges, crests, and decorative frames.

**Motion approach**
Subtle or era-specific. Gentle parallax, paper-flip effects, or VHS-style glitches depending on the decade. Avoid overdoing the novelty -- one or two signature motions are enough. Static design is also perfectly valid.

**Reference sites**
- mailchimp.com (earlier brand identity)
- dropbox.com/paper (retro elements)
- poolsuite.net

### Bold Maximalist

**Description**
Vibrant, loud, and unapologetically attention-grabbing. Rules exist to be broken. Clashing colors, oversized type, layered compositions, and sensory overload are features, not bugs. This profile is for brands that refuse to whisper.

**Best for**
Music festivals and live events, street fashion, youth culture brands, creative agencies, social media campaigns, food delivery and fast casual dining.

**Typography direction**
Oversized, ultra-bold, and compressed typefaces (Druk, Impact, Anton, Bebas Neue). Multiple typefaces and styles mixed freely -- bold sans next to thin serif next to display script. Text as visual element, not just information carrier. Variable fonts with extreme weight and width axes.

**Color mood**
Maximum saturation, maximum contrast. Clashing complementary colors (magenta and lime, orange and purple). Gradients are bold and multicolored. Neon on dark, dark on neon. No neutral backgrounds -- every surface carries color. If in doubt, add more.

**Spacing density**
Dense. Overlapping elements, text running off edges, layered compositions. Negative space is minimal. The design should feel packed with energy. When spacing exists, it is exaggerated for dramatic contrast.

**Imagery style**
Collage, cutout photography, halftone overlays, sticker aesthetics. Mixed media -- photo combined with illustration combined with pattern. Everything layered. Bright, high-saturation photography. Nothing subtle. Iconography is bold, filled, and often custom.

**Motion approach**
Highly expressive. Fast, punchy transitions. Scroll-jacking (used intentionally). Text animations, color shifts, scale changes on interaction. Kinetic typography. The experience is an event, not a brochure.

**Reference sites**
- spotify.design
- complexcon.com
- awwwards.com (the site itself)

### Corporate Trust

**Description**
Professional, reliable, and built to inspire institutional confidence. The design communicates stability, competence, and trustworthiness. It is not flashy -- it is the visual equivalent of a firm handshake and a well-tailored suit.

**Best for**
Banking and financial services, insurance, law firms, enterprise software, government services, healthcare systems, consulting firms.

**Typography direction**
Established, professional sans-serifs (IBM Plex Sans, Source Sans Pro, Open Sans, Segoe UI). Conservative sizing -- nothing oversized. Medium weights for headings, regular for body. Occasionally a serif for headings to signal tradition and authority. Consistent, systematic type scales.

**Color mood**
Conservative and trustworthy. Navy blue, deep teal, or forest green as primary. Grays and whites for backgrounds. Accent colors are muted and purposeful -- a controlled blue or green. Avoid trendy palettes. The colors should feel stable and timeless, not exciting.

**Spacing density**
Balanced to compact. Information density is higher -- users are task-oriented. Clear visual hierarchy, well-defined sections. Card-based layouts with consistent padding. Tables and data are presented cleanly. Nothing feels wasteful or overly decorative.

**Imagery style**
Professional photography of real people in business contexts. Diverse, natural poses -- not stock-photo stiff. Abstract geometric patterns for backgrounds. Data visualizations are clean and accurate. Iconography is simple, outline-style, from a consistent system. No illustrations that feel too casual.

**Motion approach**
Minimal and functional. Smooth transitions for page changes and modals. Loading indicators are simple spinners or progress bars. Micro-interactions are restrained -- a subtle highlight on hover, a smooth expand/collapse. Nothing that could be perceived as frivolous.

**Reference sites**
- stripe.com/enterprise
- wise.com
- ibm.com

### Startup Fresh

**Description**
Modern, energetic, and forward-thinking. This profile captures the optimism and momentum of early-stage companies. The design is clean but not cold, polished but not corporate. It says "we are building the future, and it is going to be great."

**Best for**
SaaS startups, developer tools, API products, mobile-first apps, crowdfunding campaigns, landing pages, product launches.

**Typography direction**
Modern geometric sans-serifs with personality (General Sans, Satoshi, Cabinet Grotesk, Manrope). Semi-bold to bold headings that feel confident. Body text is clean and readable at 16-18px. One typeface used consistently -- simplicity signals focus.

**Color mood**
Clean and bright. White or very light gray backgrounds. A single vibrant primary color -- electric blue, vivid purple, bright coral, or energetic green. Secondary colors are muted or analogous. Subtle gradients are common, especially in hero sections. The palette feels optimistic and forward-looking.

**Spacing density**
Spacious. Generous padding in hero sections, clear separation between content blocks. As the page moves toward detail (features, pricing), density increases slightly. Overall: the design breathes and does not feel rushed.

**Imagery style**
Product screenshots with subtle shadows and browser chrome. Abstract gradient shapes or blurred blobs as decorative backgrounds. Custom illustrations in a clean, flat style with limited color. Isometric or 3D-lite renders for feature visualization. Photography is minimal -- the product is the hero.

**Motion approach**
Polished and modern. Smooth scroll-triggered animations, gentle fade-and-slide reveals, interactive product demos. Hover states are responsive and satisfying. Hero sections may include subtle ambient motion (floating elements, gradient shifts). Loading transitions are fast and crisp.

**Reference sites**
- vercel.com
- linear.app
- raycast.com
