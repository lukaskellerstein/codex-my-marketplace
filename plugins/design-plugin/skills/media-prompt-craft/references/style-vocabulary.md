# Style Vocabulary

A comprehensive visual vocabulary for crafting image prompts. Each category lists terms with enough context to know when and why to use them. The more precise your vocabulary, the closer the output matches your vision.

---

## Lighting

Lighting is the single most impactful variable in any image. It sets mood, directs attention, and determines whether an image feels professional or amateur. Always specify lighting in your prompts.

### Natural Light

| Term | Description | Use when |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Golden hour | Warm, low-angle sunlight in the hour after sunrise or before sunset | You want warmth, romance, or aspirational lifestyle feel |
| Blue hour | Cool, diffused twilight light just before sunrise or after sunset | You want calm sophistication or moodiness |
| Overcast | Soft, even light from cloud-covered sky, no harsh shadows | You want neutral, flattering portraits or product shots |
| Dappled | Light filtering through leaves or lattice, creating spots of light and shadow | You want organic, natural, textured atmosphere |
| Harsh noon | Direct overhead sunlight creating strong shadows | You want high contrast, dramatic, raw feel |
| Window light | Soft directional light from a window, falls off into shadow | You want intimate, painterly, editorial feel |
| Diffused | Light scattered through translucent material (curtains, clouds) | You want soft, even illumination without drama |
| Reflected | Light bouncing off surfaces (water, walls, snow) | You want fill light or interesting color casts |
| Backlit silhouette | Subject between camera and light source, mostly in shadow | You want mystery, drama, or anonymity |

### Studio Light

| Term | Description | Use when |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Rembrandt lighting | Angled key light creating a triangle of light on the shadow-side cheek | You want classic, dramatic portraits with depth |
| Butterfly lighting | Light positioned directly above and in front, creating shadow under nose | You want glamorous, beauty-style portraits |
| Rim lighting | Light from behind the subject, creating a bright edge outline | You want subject separation from background or a halo effect |
| Split lighting | Light hitting exactly half the face, other half in shadow | You want maximum drama or duality themes |
| Broad lighting | Key light on the side of face closest to camera | You want to widen narrow faces or create openness |
| Fill light | Secondary light reducing shadow intensity | You want to soften contrast and reveal shadow detail |
| Hair light | Light specifically targeting hair from above/behind | You want depth and separation in portraits |
| Clamshell lighting | Two lights: one above, one below, creating even wrap | You want commercial beauty or product shots |
| High key | Bright, even lighting with minimal shadows | You want clean, airy, optimistic feel |
| Low key | Predominantly dark with selective lighting | You want moody, dramatic, mysterious feel |

### Mood Lighting

| Term | Description | Use when |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Dramatic | Strong contrast between light and shadow | You want intensity, power, or importance |
| Ethereal | Soft, glowing, slightly overexposed light | You want dreaminess or spiritual quality |
| Moody | Underexposed with pools of light | You want atmosphere, intrigue, or intimacy |
| Soft | Low contrast, gentle gradations | You want approachability and comfort |
| Harsh | High contrast with sharp shadow edges | You want energy, confrontation, or rawness |
| Cinematic | Motivated, directional, often warm/cool mixed | You want storytelling quality and production value |
| Neon | Colored artificial light, often pink/blue/green | You want urban, nightlife, or cyberpunk feel |
| Candlelight | Warm, flickering, very localized | You want intimacy, tradition, or romance |
| Volumetric | Visible light rays through atmosphere (fog, dust) | You want depth, atmosphere, or the supernatural |

### Direction

| Term | Use when |
|------|----------|
| Backlit | You want silhouettes, halos, or lens flare |
| Side-lit | You want to reveal texture and form |
| Top-lit | You want overhead natural feel or spotlight drama |
| Under-lit | You want unease, horror, or campfire stories |
| Front-lit | You want flat, even, documentary feel |
| Cross-lit | You want maximum texture and dimension |

## Composition

Composition terms tell the AI or guide the photographer on how to arrange elements within the frame. Good composition makes an image feel intentional.

| Term | Description | Use when |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Rule of thirds | Subject placed at intersection of thirds grid | You want balanced but dynamic framing |
| Golden ratio | Spiral-based placement, mathematically harmonious | You want organic, naturally pleasing balance |
| Centered / symmetrical | Subject dead center, balanced left and right | You want power, stability, or formality |
| Negative space | Large empty areas surrounding the subject | You want breathing room, minimalism, or text placement |
| Leading lines | Lines in the scene directing eye toward subject | You want guided attention and depth |
| Frame within frame | Doorways, windows, arches framing the subject | You want layered depth and context |
| Bird's eye view | Looking straight down from above | You want patterns, scale, or novelty |
| Worm's eye view | Looking straight up from ground level | You want power, monumentality, or awe |
| Dutch angle | Camera tilted to one side | You want tension, unease, or energy |
| Symmetry | Mirror-image balance across a central axis | You want order, calm, or formality |
| Asymmetry | Intentional imbalance in visual weight | You want dynamism, tension, or modernity |
| Diagonal | Key elements arranged on diagonal lines | You want energy, movement, or instability |
| Layered foreground | Distinct fore/mid/background planes | You want depth and immersion |
| Shallow depth of field | Sharp subject, blurred background | You want focus isolation and intimacy |
| Deep focus | Everything sharp from near to far | You want detail, context, or documentary feel |
| Close crop | Tight framing cutting into the subject | You want intimacy, detail, or abstraction |
| Wide establishing | Broad view showing full environment | You want context, scale, or grandeur |
| Over the shoulder | Camera behind one person looking at another | You want conversational intimacy and POV |
| Flat lay | Objects arranged on flat surface, shot from above | You want product displays, organization, or lifestyle |
| Panoramic | Extra-wide aspect ratio | You want sweep, landscape, or cinematic scope |

## Color Descriptors

Color terms shape the entire mood and era of an image. Be specific about color relationships, not just individual colors.

| Term | Description | Use when |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Warm | Reds, oranges, yellows dominate | You want energy, comfort, or nostalgia |
| Cool | Blues, greens, purples dominate | You want calm, professionalism, or distance |
| Muted | Reduced saturation, grayed-out | You want sophistication, vintage, or subtlety |
| Saturated | Full, vivid color intensity | You want energy, youth, or boldness |
| Pastel | Light, soft, low-saturation tints | You want gentleness, femininity, or spring |
| Neon | Bright, electric, artificial colors | You want nightlife, tech, or edginess |
| Monochromatic | Single hue in varying tones | You want cohesion, elegance, or focus |
| Complementary | Opposite colors on the color wheel | You want contrast, vibrance, or pop |
| Analogous | Adjacent colors on the color wheel | You want harmony and flow |
| Earth tones | Browns, tans, olives, rusts | You want natural, grounded, organic feel |
| Jewel tones | Deep, rich: emerald, sapphire, ruby, amethyst | You want luxury, richness, or opulence |
| Desaturated | Intentionally drained of color | You want bleakness, vintage, or clinical feel |
| High contrast | Large difference between lights and darks | You want drama, readability, or boldness |
| Low contrast | Small difference between tones | You want softness, dreaminess, or cohesion |
| Two-tone | Only two colors used | You want graphic impact or retro feel |
| Gradient | Smooth transition between colors | You want modernity, depth, or fluidity |
| Ombre | Gradient typically from dark to light (or vice versa) | You want fashion-forward or organic transition |
| Teal and orange | Classic cinematic color grade | You want film-quality, Hollywood look |
| Black and white | No color, full grayscale | You want timelessness, drama, or fine art feel |
| Sepia | Warm brownish monochrome | You want nostalgia, history, or warmth |
| Cross-processed | Shifted, unnatural colors (greens in shadows, magentas in highlights) | You want retro film, lo-fi, or experimental feel |
| Color-blocked | Flat areas of solid color with sharp boundaries | You want graphic, modern, or bold pop art feel |
| Duotone | Image rendered in two specific colors | You want graphic, modern, brand-aligned treatment |
| Split-tone | Different color in highlights vs shadows | You want cinematic polish or vintage film look |
| Achromatic | True neutrals: black, white, gray only | You want pure minimalism with no color distraction |
| Iridescent | Shifting rainbow-like color on surfaces | You want futuristic, magical, or premium feel |
| Metallic | Gold, silver, copper, bronze color quality | You want luxury, industrial, or premium material feel |
| Acid | Bright yellows, greens, magentas | You want rave culture, rebellion, or extreme energy |
| Faded | Colors look sun-bleached or aged | You want vintage, nostalgic, or well-worn feel |

| Editorial | Magazine-quality photography with intentional styling | You want fashion, food, or lifestyle premium content |

## Quality Modifiers

Quality modifiers are technical instructions that control resolution, lens behavior, and post-processing characteristics. They make the difference between amateur and professional-looking output.

| Term | Effect | Use when |
|------|--------|----------|
| High resolution | Maximum detail and pixel count | Always include for production-quality images |
| 4K | 3840x2160 pixel output | You need large format or retina-quality images |
| 8K | 7680x4320 pixel output | You need extreme detail for cropping or large prints |
| Sharp focus | Crisp detail on the subject | You want clarity on the main subject |
| Shallow depth of field | Blurred background, sharp subject | You want subject isolation and cinematic feel |
| Wide angle | Broad field of view, slight distortion at edges | You want environmental context or dramatic perspective |
| Telephoto | Compressed perspective, narrow field of view | You want flattened depth or close-up from distance |
| Macro | Extreme close-up revealing tiny details | You want texture, small objects, or abstract crops |
| Tilt-shift | Selective focus plane creating miniature effect | You want toylike look or architectural correction |
| Long exposure | Motion blur from extended shutter time | You want light trails, smooth water, or movement |
| HDR | Wide dynamic range, detail in shadows and highlights | You want balanced exposure in high-contrast scenes |
| Film grain | Analog noise pattern from film emulsion | You want vintage, cinematic, or textured quality |
| Lens flare | Light artifacts from shooting toward light source | You want warmth, energy, or cinematic feel |
| Bokeh | Out-of-focus light rendered as soft circles | You want dreamy background or festive atmosphere |
| Shot on 35mm | 35mm film aesthetic and aspect ratio | You want classic documentary or street photography feel |
| Shot on medium format | Larger sensor look: shallower DOF, higher detail | You want fashion, portrait, or fine art quality |
| Anamorphic | Cinematic wide format with horizontal lens flare | You want Hollywood production quality |
