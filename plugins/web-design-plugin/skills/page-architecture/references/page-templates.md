# Page Templates by Business Type

Common page types with their typical section compositions. Each template lists sections in conversion-funnel order, explains why the order works, and notes which sections are optional.

---

## Landing Page (Single Product/Service)

**Goal:** Convert visitors into sign-ups or leads for a single offering.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose in Funnel |
|---|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Hero | Yes | Hook — immediate value proposition and primary CTA |
| 2 | Social Proof Bar | No | Trust — logos of known companies reduce skepticism early |
| 3 | Features | Yes | Value — show what they get |
| 4 | How It Works | No | Clarity — reduce confusion about the product |
| 5 | Testimonials | Yes | Proof — real people validating the promise |
| 6 | CTA Banner | Yes | Convert — repeat the primary CTA with urgency |
| 7 | FAQ | No | Friction removal — answer objections before they become blockers |
| 8 | Footer | Yes | Navigation — legal, secondary links, social |

**Why this order works:** The hero hooks attention with a bold promise. Social proof immediately backs it up ("if these companies use it, it must be legit"). Features explain what they get. How-it-works reduces complexity anxiety. Testimonials provide emotional proof. The CTA banner catches anyone who scrolled the whole page. FAQ mops up lingering objections.

**When to trim:** For a simple launch page, drop Social Proof Bar, How It Works, and FAQ. Keep Hero → Features → Testimonials → CTA → Footer as the minimum.

## SaaS Marketing Site

**Goal:** Drive free trial sign-ups or demo requests for a software product.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose in Funnel |
|---|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Hero | Yes | Hook — headline + product screenshot or animation |
| 2 | Logo Bar | No | Trust — "Trusted by 500+ teams including..." |
| 3 | Features | Yes | Value — 3-6 key features with icons/illustrations |
| 4 | Demo/Screenshot | Yes | Proof — show the actual product in action |
| 5 | Pricing | Yes | Decision — transparent pricing removes guesswork |
| 6 | Testimonials | Yes | Validation — quotes from real users with photos |
| 7 | FAQ | No | Friction removal — billing, security, integration questions |
| 8 | CTA Banner | Yes | Convert — final push with trial CTA |
| 9 | Footer | Yes | Navigation — product, company, resources, legal links |

**Why this order works:** SaaS buyers follow a predictable journey: "What is this?" (hero) → "Who else uses it?" (logos) → "What does it do?" (features) → "Show me" (demo) → "How much?" (pricing) → "Does it actually work?" (testimonials) → "But what about..." (FAQ) → "OK, I'll try it" (CTA).

**Additional pages typically needed:**
- Individual feature pages for SEO and detail
- Integrations page
- Changelog or what's new
- Documentation/help center link

## E-commerce Product Page

**Goal:** Drive add-to-cart and purchase for a specific product.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose in Funnel |
|---|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Product Gallery | Yes | Visual proof — high-quality images from multiple angles |
| 2 | Product Info | Yes | Decision — name, price, variants, add-to-cart |
| 3 | Product Details | Yes | Information — description, specs, materials, sizing |
| 4 | Reviews | Yes | Social proof — star ratings and written reviews |
| 5 | Related Products | No | Upsell — "You might also like" cross-sell |
| 6 | Recently Viewed | No | Re-engagement — products they browsed earlier |
| 7 | Footer | Yes | Navigation — shipping info, returns, customer service |

**Why this order works:** E-commerce is image-first. The gallery and product info sit above the fold as a unified purchase decision zone. Details and reviews sit below for researchers who need more information. Related products catch users who are not convinced by this particular item.

**Key consideration:** The product info section must include price, variant selectors (size, color), quantity, and add-to-cart button visible without scrolling on desktop.

## Portfolio / Agency

**Goal:** Attract client inquiries by showcasing work quality and expertise.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose in Funnel |
|---|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Hero | Yes | Hook — bold statement of capability, possibly with reel |
| 2 | Work Showcase | Yes | Proof — curated project grid or carousel |
| 3 | Services | Yes | Clarity — what you offer, structured by category |
| 4 | About / Team | No | Connection — humanize the brand, show expertise |
| 5 | Testimonials | Yes | Validation — client quotes with company names |
| 6 | Contact | Yes | Convert — inquiry form or scheduling link |
| 7 | Footer | Yes | Navigation — social links, legal, secondary pages |

**Why this order works:** Agencies sell on the strength of their portfolio. The hero sets the tone ("we do exceptional work"), the work showcase immediately proves it, services clarify what they can hire you for, and testimonials from past clients seal the deal. The contact section is the conversion point.

**Portfolio-specific tips:**
- Show 4-8 best projects, not everything
- Each project card should show: thumbnail, client name, project type, brief result
- Individual case study pages are more effective than cramming details on the home page

## Blog / Magazine

**Goal:** Drive readership, newsletter subscriptions, and ad impressions.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose in Funnel |
|---|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Featured Post | Yes | Hook — large hero-style card for the top story |
| 2 | Category Grid | No | Navigation — visual links to content categories |
| 3 | Recent Posts | Yes | Content — chronological feed of latest articles |
| 4 | Newsletter CTA | Yes | Convert — email subscription with value proposition |
| 5 | Popular / Trending | No | Engagement — most-read articles for social proof |
| 6 | Footer | Yes | Navigation — categories, about, legal, social |

**Why this order works:** Content sites need to surface the best content immediately. The featured post acts as a hero, drawing readers into the most important piece. Category navigation helps returning users find their interests. Recent posts serve the "what's new" impulse. The newsletter CTA converts casual readers into subscribers.

**Content page (individual article) structure:**
- Article header (title, author, date, reading time, category)
- Article body (rich text with images, code blocks, pull quotes)
- Author bio
- Related articles
- Newsletter CTA
- Comments (optional)

## Dashboard

**Goal:** Provide actionable data and task management for logged-in users.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose |
|---|---------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Sidebar Navigation | Yes | Navigation — app sections, collapsible on mobile |
| 2 | Header | Yes | Context — page title, search, user menu, notifications |
| 3 | Stats Cards | Yes | Overview — 3-5 key metrics at a glance |
| 4 | Charts | No | Analysis — visual data trends (line, bar, pie) |
| 5 | Data Table | No | Detail — sortable, filterable records |
| 6 | Activity Feed | No | Awareness — recent actions, updates, alerts |

**Why this order works:** Dashboards follow an information hierarchy from overview to detail. Stats cards give the instant pulse check. Charts reveal trends. Tables provide the raw data for deep dives. Activity feeds keep users aware of changes.

**Dashboard-specific considerations:**
- Every metric should show comparison (vs. last period, vs. target)
- Empty states need design for new users with no data
- Loading skeletons are essential for data-heavy views
- Responsive: sidebar collapses to hamburger on mobile

## Corporate / Enterprise

**Goal:** Generate leads from enterprise buyers through credibility and thought leadership.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose in Funnel |
|---|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Hero | Yes | Hook — enterprise-grade value proposition |
| 2 | Trusted By | Yes | Trust — logos of Fortune 500 or industry-leading clients |
| 3 | Solutions | Yes | Value — categorized by industry, use case, or team size |
| 4 | Case Studies | Yes | Proof — quantified results from named customers |
| 5 | Resources | No | Education — whitepapers, webinars, reports (lead magnets) |
| 6 | Contact / Sales | Yes | Convert — "Talk to Sales" form or scheduling widget |
| 7 | Footer | Yes | Navigation — products, solutions, company, legal, compliance |

**Why this order works:** Enterprise buyers need heavy credibility signals. Logo bars and case studies do the heavy lifting. Solutions sections help buyers self-identify their use case. Resources serve the long sales cycle by providing downloadable content in exchange for contact info.

## Startup / Launch Page

**Goal:** Build a waitlist and generate buzz before product launch.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose in Funnel |
|---|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Hero | Yes | Hook — bold problem statement + "Coming soon" energy |
| 2 | Problem / Solution | Yes | Relevance — articulate the pain, then the fix |
| 3 | Features | No | Preview — tease key capabilities |
| 4 | Team | No | Trust — founders' credibility and backgrounds |
| 5 | Investors / Press | No | Validation — logos, quotes, or funding announcements |
| 6 | Waitlist CTA | Yes | Convert — email capture with incentive ("Be first") |
| 7 | Footer | Yes | Navigation — minimal, social links, legal |

**Why this order works:** Pre-launch pages sell a vision, not a product. The problem/solution section is critical — it proves you understand the pain. Features tease without over-promising. Team and investor sections build trust that this will actually ship. The waitlist CTA with a position counter or incentive creates urgency.

**Keep it lean:** Pre-launch pages should be a single page. Do not create multiple pages for a product that does not exist yet.

## Restaurant / Local Business

**Goal:** Drive reservations, orders, or foot traffic.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose in Funnel |
|---|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Hero | Yes | Hook — stunning food/ambiance photography |
| 2 | Menu Highlights | Yes | Value — featured dishes with photos and prices |
| 3 | About | No | Connection — chef's story, restaurant history |
| 4 | Gallery | No | Atmosphere — interior, food, events photos |
| 5 | Reservations | Yes | Convert — booking widget or phone/link |
| 6 | Location / Hours | Yes | Practical — map, address, opening hours, parking |
| 7 | Footer | Yes | Navigation — social, phone, email, legal |

**Why this order works:** Restaurant visitors want to see the food first, then figure out logistics. The hero with professional food photography does the emotional selling. Menu highlights help decide "Is this the kind of food I want?" Reservations and location/hours answer "How do I get there and when?"

**Local business adaptations:**
- Replace Menu Highlights with Services for salons, gyms, etc.
- Add a Reviews section pulling from Google/Yelp for businesses where ratings matter
- Ensure phone number is click-to-call on mobile

## Event / Conference

**Goal:** Sell tickets and attract attendees.

| # | Section | Required | Purpose in Funnel |
|---|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Hero with Date | Yes | Hook — event name, date, location, countdown timer |
| 2 | Speakers | Yes | Value — headliner speakers with photos and bios |
| 3 | Schedule | Yes | Information — day-by-day or track-by-track agenda |
| 4 | Venue | No | Practical — location, map, travel info, accommodations |
| 5 | Sponsors | No | Credibility — sponsor logos by tier |
| 6 | Tickets CTA | Yes | Convert — pricing tiers, early-bird deadlines |
| 7 | FAQ | No | Friction removal — refund policy, COVID, accessibility |
| 8 | Footer | Yes | Navigation — social, contact, code of conduct, legal |

**Why this order works:** Events sell on speakers and FOMO. The hero with a countdown timer creates urgency. Speakers are the headline act — they are why people buy tickets. The schedule proves there is substance beyond the headliners. Ticket pricing with early-bird deadlines drives immediate conversion.

**Event-specific considerations:**
- The hero MUST include the date prominently — it is the single most important detail
- Countdown timer creates urgency
- Past event photos/video build credibility for recurring events
- Mobile experience is critical — attendees will reference the schedule on their phones
